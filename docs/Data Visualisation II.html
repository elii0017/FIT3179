Australia's Corrective Services

This dashboard explores data on Australia's corrective services, visualising trends in imprisonment rates, prisoner demographics

Australians in Custody

In the recent June quarter, 44,051 individuals were in custody. An increase of 2% of 746 more individuals since the March quarter.

Whilst the graph appears to "fluctuate" due to the y-axis starting at 40,000, this was done to better visualise the increase and decrease of custody numbers.

The lowest amount of Australians in custody between the periods of 2019 to 2024 was 40,330 in the March Quarter of 2022

Australia's Daily Imprisonment Rate

Australia's Jun Qtr 2024 Imprisonment Rate was 208 persons per 100,000 in the adult population.

The Northern Territory has the highest imprisonment rate (>1000) out of any other state or territory

The map does not display the change in March Qtr 2024 or June Qtr 2024 as it does not adequately display the change in daily imprisonment rates across each state.

2016-2024 Prisoner Reception

Click on the legend below to highlight a line

The drop in 2024 is due to data only available up to June.

The States of New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland appear to have the highest amount of prisoner receptions, this could be due to these states having a higher population compared to the rest.

In general the Australian males, in particular non-indigenous males have the highest prisoner receptions. This trend is noted for almost all states and territories besides the Northern Territory. The Northern Territory experiences more prisoner receptions by Indigenous people.

Some data was not published for certain quarters, as such the graph may show a larger decrease in a year

Proportions of Male and Female Prisoners

Click on legend to rearrange the graph

Although there are 92% of males(40,578) and 8% of females(3,473) in prison, by normalising the data, we can see that the age brackets of female and male prisoners have similar trends.

The age brackets of 25-34 and 35-44 appears to account for roughly 60% to 65% of most prisoners.

As the age bracket increases we observe a noticeable drop in percentages. This decrease could be due to prisoners being released after serving their time or that it is less likely for people significantly older to commit crimes.

Indigenous to Non-Indigenous Prisoner Proportion

From a quick glance we can observe that there are more Non-Indigenous than Indigenous prisoners. We can see that from 2023 there was an increase of 1,694 Indigenous prisoners and a decrease of 997 Non-Indigenous prisoners. Demographic Breakdown of Age Brackets in Custody

By breaking it down further we can see that amongst Indigenous and Non-Indigenous prisoners that a similar trend exists for the age brackets. What we can observe is that there are more crimes committed by youthful Indigenous between the ages of 18-24.
